Barangay Justice Study

Documented Conflict Cases in Libertad

A descriptive study examining the types, frequency, and resolution procedures of conflicts processed under the Katarungang Pambarangay system.

Official Records

Barangay case files only

Descriptive Study

No surveys or interviews

Resolution Methods

Mediation and conciliation

Community Focus

Libertad, Misamis Oriental

This study examines conflict cases officially recorded in selected barangays of Libertad, Misamis Oriental using existing barangay documentation.

Conflicts within communities are a normal aspect of everyday social life. In barangays, residents live close together, share public spaces, and interact regularly. These conditions make disputes over family matters, property boundaries, noise disturbances, and interpersonal disagreements more likely.

To address these issues, the Government of the Philippines institutionalized the Katarungang Pambarangay (KP) under Republic Act No. 7160 to provide accessible, community-based dispute resolution.

The KP process is led by the Punong Barangay and the Lupon Tagapamayapa, who facilitate settlements and ensure proper documentation.

Programs such as the Lupong Tagapamayapa Incentives Awards (LTIA) highlight best practices in documentation and dispute resolution.

Studying documented conflict cases in the barangays of Libertad provides insight into local governance effectiveness and community justice implementation.
